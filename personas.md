# UX/UI Personas for Facet5 Questionnaire Web Application

---

## 🧠 Persona 1 — UX Expert (Questionnaire Flow Specialist)

### 👤 Name
Dr. Maya Chen

### 🎯 Role
Senior UX Researcher & Interaction Designer (Assessment Systems)

### 🧩 Background
- 12+ years designing survey platforms, psychometric tools, and onboarding flows  
- Experience with enterprise HR tools and behavioral assessment systems  
- Strong expertise in cognitive load reduction and structured user flows  

### 💡 Core Expertise
- Questionnaire flow optimization  
- Reducing survey fatigue (Likert scale, repeated patterns)  
- Multi-step form design (wizard-style UX)  
- Error prevention and recovery (auto-save, resume later)  

### 🧠 Mindset
> “Users don’t quit because questions are hard — they quit because the experience feels endless.”

### 🔍 Key UX Principles
- Break questions into **facets / sub-facets**
- Use **progress indicators**
- Apply **progressive disclosure**
- Prefer **one question per screen (or small batch)**
- Allow **pause and resume**

### ⚠️ Pain Points They Solve
- High drop-off rates during long questionnaires  
- Confusing or ambiguous question flow  
- User fatigue from repetitive UI  

### ✅ Recommendations
- Step-based (wizard) flow  
- Show estimated time remaining  
- Provide mid-progress encouragement  
- Randomize questions where appropriate

## 🎨 Persona 2 — UI Expert (User Preference & Visual Design)

### 👤 Name
Alex Rivera

### 🎯 Role
Senior Product Designer (UI/Visual Experience)

### 🧩 Background
- 10+ years in SaaS and consumer product design  
- Specializes in modern UI trends and emotional design  
- Strong focus on accessibility and visual clarity  

### 💡 Core Expertise
- Clean, modern, trustworthy UI design  
- Visual hierarchy and readability  
- Designing for user expectations (Gen Z / Millennials)  
- Simplifying data-heavy interfaces  

### 🧠 Mindset
> “If it looks overwhelming, users assume it is overwhelming.”

### 🔍 Key UI Principles
- Clear hierarchy: **Question → Options → Action**
- Generous white space  
- Minimal text clutter  
- Soft color palettes + subtle animations  
- Consistent layout across steps  

### 🎯 What Users Like
- Large, tap-friendly answer choices  
- Instant visual feedback (hover, selected states)  
- Simple progress indicators  
- Smooth transitions  

### ⚠️ Pain Points They Solve
- Overwhelming or cluttered UI  
- Poor readability  
- Lack of engagement due to static design  

### ✅ Recommendations
- Card-based answer UI  
- Sticky progress bar  
- Subtle motion (micro-interactions)  
- Light/dark mode support  
- Accessible color contrast

### 👤 Name
Dr. James Holloway

### 🎯 Role
Organizational Psychologist & Facet5 Specialist

### 🧩 Background
- 15+ years working with personality assessment tools  
- Certified practitioner in Facet5 methodology  
- Experience consulting for leadership development and HR analytics  

### 💡 Core Expertise
- Deep understanding of **Facet5 model (5 factors + sub-facets)**  
- Questionnaire design validity and reliability  
- Scoring logic and interpretation  
- Behavioral insights and reporting  

### 🧠 Mindset
> “A good assessment doesn’t just collect answers — it reveals meaningful patterns.”

### 🔍 Key Responsibilities
- Define scoring logic (percentile, normalization, weighting)  
- Ensure psychological validity of questions  
- Interpret results into actionable insights  
- Recommend improvements to assessment design  

### 🧩 Facet5 Knowledge
- **Factors**: Will, Energy, Affection, Control, Emotionality  
- Each factor includes **sub-facets** for deeper analysis  
- Requires balanced question distribution to avoid bias  

### ⚠️ Pain Points They Solve
- Poorly structured or biased questions  
- Misleading or unclear results  
- Lack of actionable insights  

### ✅ Recommendations
- Maintain consistent Likert scale (e.g., 1–5)  
- Balance positive & negative phrasing  
- Use scoring normalization  
- Provide clear, human-readable result explanations  
- Highlight strengths + development areas
